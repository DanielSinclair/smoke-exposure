# Smoke Exposure graphics

Ten reproducible 2400×1350 graphics for social sharing and editorial use. Each image is generated from `data/processed/dashboard.json` by `pipeline/render_social_graphics.py`; facts, dates, and source boundaries therefore stay aligned with the report.

1. `01-modeled-smoke-trend.png` — annual modeled exposure and its five-year trend, 2006–2023
2. `02-monthly-smoke-history.png` — monthly evidence for the 30 years with available documentary or national data
3. `03-worst-smoke-days.png` — the ten modeled days with the broadest U.S. reach
4. `04-burned-area-trendlines.png` — complete Canadian and U.S. burned-area trendlines
5. `05-recent-smoke-comparison.png` — same-window monitor-and-satellite comparison through July 18
6. `06-seasonality-shift.png` — changing calendar-month concentration of modeled exposure
7. `07-regional-concentration.png` — the five highest-burden states in 2023
8. `08-historical-review-coverage.png` — pre-2006 source-review coverage and accepted episodes
9. `09-fire-record-coverage.png` — annual U.S. and Canadian fire-catalog coverage
10. `10-2026-fire-activity.png` — preliminary 2026 fires and acres burned through July 18

The documentary, modeled, monitor-and-satellite, and fire-activity products remain separate. Blank historical coverage means unknown, not zero.
